How to Play:

Game Objectives

The goal of the game is to achieve the following victory conditions:

1. Line up your tokens in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row of 4.

2. Place your tokens in a 2 x 2 square.

3. Block your opponents so that they have no more legal moves remaining.

Game Play

You have the option to choose to play a 1 player game or a 2 player game. If you select the 1 player game option, you will play against the computer. The 2 player game option will allow you to play with one other person.

There are 16 tiles, and each is composed of 2 elements, one fruit (banana, orange, strawberry, watermelon) and one animal friend (dog, dragon, kangaroo, turtle). The tiles are randomly arranged in a 4x4 grid to form the game board.

The game is turn-based. On your turn, select a tile to place your token on top of. Once a tile is selected, it is unplayable for the remainder of the game. If you are the first player, you must choose a tile that is on the outside border of the game board. This is only required for the first turn of the game. The next player must then place a token on a tile that shares an element in common with the tile that was selected in the previous turn.

On the following turns, continue to place tokens on tiles that have a common element with the tile selected by the previous player, while trying to arrange your tokens in one of the three victory conditions. The first player to achieve a victory condition wins the game!

End of the Game

The game ends when a player achieves one of the following victory conditions:

1. A vertical, horizontal or diagonal row of tokens.

2. A 2 x 2 square of 4 tokens.

3. Blocking an opponent so that they have no legal moves remaining.

In this example, the last tile selected has a dog and watermelon on it. Since there are no more tiles remaining with either of those elements, Player 2 wins this game.
